# ANC Proposal Engine

Live at [proposals.anc.com](https://proposals.anc.com) (internal: `abc-ancapp.izcgmb.easypanel.host`).

The Proposal Engine is where the sales side of ANC lives. Everything that turns a client conversation into a signed deal — pipeline tracking, pricing, estimating, scope of work, installation planning, submittal docs — runs through this tool.

## Who uses it

| Role | What they do here |
|------|------|
| **Sales team** (Paul, Jireh, BD team) | Build pipeline, draft proposals, send to clients |
| **Estimators** (Natalia and team) | Price deals, generate SOWs, handle revisions |
| **Project managers** | Move won deals into execution — submittals, install plans |
| **Execs** | Review the pipeline, approve pricing, check win/loss |

## What it does, at a high level

- **Pipeline tracking** — every active, won, and lost opportunity in one place
- **Estimator** — turns requirements into priced line items; handles revisions and alternates
- **Proposals** — branded PDFs clients actually receive
- **Scope of Work (SOW) generation** — AI-assisted installation SOWs and premium SOWs
- **Project handoff** — when a deal closes, it becomes a Project with submittal tracking and install workflows
- **RFP Analyzer** — point it at a client's RFP, get a structured response with compliance matrix
- **Submittal Compiler** — rolls specs, cut sheets, and drawings into the submittal package the installer actually uses

## Relationship to the rest of ANC

- **Twenty CRM** (crm.ancsports.net) is the system of record for Companies, People, and Opportunities. Every PDF, SOW, or Excel export from the Proposal Engine pushes back into Twenty automatically — the Opportunity gets an attachment + a timeline activity. This is the **Universal CRM Push**.
- **Service Dashboard** (services.ancsports.net) takes over once a deal is won. Venues, screens, and service events all flow from "this was sold" to "this is how we operate it."

In short: proposals start here, land in the CRM, and then flow into the service dashboard when they go live.
